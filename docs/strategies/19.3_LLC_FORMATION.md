# MS19 Phase 3 Strategy — LLC Formation

Status: In progress. CyberCreek LLC (parent) approved 2026-06-17 (AZCC Business ID 25076646); CryptoZing subsidiary filing is next.
Parent milestone doc: [`docs/milestones/19_OB_HARDENING_OPS.md`](../milestones/19_OB_HARDENING_OPS.md)

**Goal:** Stand up the entity structure that backs the CryptoZing service: a parent **CyberCreek LLC** holding a subsidiary **CryptoZing LLC**. The entity provides the liability shield that ToS protections rely on; without it, ToS clauses don't actually shield the operator personally. Phase covers both formations, EINs, a business bank account, and operating agreements so the structure is operationally complete, not just paperwork.

(Disclaimer: this strategy doc captures common solo-SaaS LLC formation steps and is not legal or tax advice. If anything in this phase touches a question that genuinely warrants a professional, surface it instead of pushing through.)

## Structure & timing

CyberCreek LLC is formed **first**, then CryptoZing LLC is formed **with CyberCreek LLC as its sole member** — building the parent-subsidiary structure from the start to avoid a later membership-interest transfer. CyberCreek LLC's sole member is Nate Barlow.

Sequence is serial: CryptoZing's filing can't name CyberCreek as member until CyberCreek exists — which it now does (approved 2026-06-17). CryptoZing's Articles can be filed immediately; AZCC approved CyberCreek in ~9 days, so CryptoZing's approval (the MS21 name-lock) should land well ahead of the swap. CyberCreek's own publication runs in parallel on its 60-day clock (deadline 2026-08-07) and does not gate CryptoZing's filing.

**Timing watch-item, not a blocker:** Full LLC formation must land within MS19 (by 2026-08-01); MS21's deploy-time entity-name swap starts 2026-08-15. File the CryptoZing subsidiary now: ~9-day AZCC approval + ~3-week publication lands its formation in early August — within the MS19 window and ahead of the swap. The swap only needs the CryptoZing LLC name *locked* (AZCC Articles approved), which lands well before 8/15; full publication can trail as a compliance step. The parallel-track design insulates MS19 regardless: Phase 5 drafts ToS/Privacy with placeholder names, and nothing in MS19 close waits on formation.

## Decisions made

- **Entity structure:** Parent **CyberCreek LLC** (sole member: Nate Barlow) → subsidiary **CryptoZing LLC** (sole member: CyberCreek LLC). Formed parent-first deliberately to avoid a later membership-interest transfer. CyberCreek is the long-term home for multiple projects.
- **State of formation:** Arizona (home state) for both. Operating from AZ; remote-SaaS customers in other states don't trigger nexus requiring foreign-LLC registration at beta volumes.
- **Entity type:** Both single-member LLCs.
- **Tax treatment:** Both default to disregarded-entity status — CryptoZing LLC is disregarded to its member CyberCreek LLC, which is in turn disregarded to Nate, so income still flows to personal Schedule C. No tax change vs. a sole proprietorship at this stage. Revisit an S-corp election (likely at the CyberCreek level) if profits cross the typical breakeven threshold (~$70–100K profit).
- **Statutory agent:** Self for both (operating from an AZ address satisfies the requirement).
- **County:** Mohave County for both. Publication requirement applies to each (only Maricopa and Pima are exempt from AZ's publication rule), so **two** 3-week newspaper notices. Budget ~$30–150 per notice.
- **Business bank account:** America First Credit Union. Primary operating account opens in **CryptoZing LLC's** name (it's the revenue entity) once its Articles + EIN are in hand. A separate CyberCreek account is deferred until there's a reason for parent-level banking.
- **E&O / Professional Liability Insurance:** Deferred — revenue-gated, not milestone-gated. Bind only after CryptoZing generates revenue that justifies the cost. Don't fund it from day-job income.
